Atlantis Hub

As part of my Interaction Design Studio class, my team and I were tasked with creating a data-driven environmental display for the fictional Atlantis Transportation Hub. We designed an interactive experience for travelers and residents traveling between the San Juan islands.

ROLES:  UI/UX Design, Prototyping, Animation
TEAM:  Matt Franklin, Jackie Hu, Kitty Bian
DURATION:  1 month
TOOLS:  Figma, Adobe Photoshop, Laser Cutting
FOCUS:  Data Analysis, Personas, Controls, Motion, Wireframing

BACKGROUND

The Atlantis Hub terminal display is a realtime data-driven environmental display located in the San Juan Islands transportation hub. The display provides information about planes, trains, and ferries, and a local unlimited ride pass option. We were given data consisting of over a hundred travel routes from the San Juan Islands, as well as three personas, consisting of the users and stakeholders. The goal of this project was to design a user-centered display to present the data in an engaging and interactive manner to address the needs of the main stakeholder and users.

CHALLENGE

Data displays in major transportation hubs often hold an abundance of information but can often be frustrating for travelers to read and understand as they look for information that is relevant to them while dealing with the stress of traveling.

OUTCOME

An interactive and accessible experience that allows users to engage with the data driven display and promotes the stakeholder's new PTF pass.

UNDERSTANDING OUR USERS AND STAKEHOLDERS

Stakeholder - Elizabeth Duarte
  • Promote the PTF Pass, a new product that offers great value and flexibility for travelers.
  • * Offer a highly readable environmental data driven display.
Local Resident - George Shimko
  • Wants to plan exciting itineraries for his grandchildren when they visit.
  • * Plan travel routes and learn how to switch between different forms of transportation through the display.
Frequent Travler - Patrick Mulvaney
  • Frequently travels between Seattle and San Juan Islands and wants to travel effectively.
  • Easily identify alternative routes in case of delays or cancellations.

SKETCHES

We each used rapid ideation to hand sketch many different possibilities of presenting the data. Through parallel design, we were able to talk through each design and as a group choose what we liked from each design and bring those features together for the next iteration.

ITERATIONS

We then digitized our sketches using Figma. We created multiple iterations followed by a critique session for each iteration. We refined our design after each critique to create a delightful experience for users while meeting our stakeholder and users needs. In addition to the display screens, we incorporated an animation and control to facilitate an interactive experience.

FINAL DESIGN

Information Architecture

The layout of the trip information is organized to make the most important data apparent at a glance. Our goal was to emphasize the trip that is leaving first and provide emphasis through a larger box, a lower opacity, larger font and more detailed information.

Map Design

Several iterations of map design were required to find a balance between the familiar features of an accurate geographic representation, with the simplicity of a diagram. Once this balance was found the map was incorporated into the overall design and style to form a cohesive display.

Control Design

Our control consists of five buttons in the shape of the islands. When users press one of the island, the screen will display the sightseeing information of the island accordingly on the right side of the screen and zoom in on the selected island.

REFLECTION

This project presented a number of challenges in terms of both the design itself, and in learning to work effectively as a team on a unified design. We each began with very different perspectives on the best way to present the information for users and stakeholders. Through the use of personas, empathy mapping, and data models, we were able to come together to create a display that combined the best elements of each of our parallel designs into a combination greater than the sum of its parts. From that foundational design, we were able to build and iterate as a group to incorporate new elements of motion and interaction based upon our own reflection and feedback we received along the way. In the end, we were able to create a display that is both credible and enjoyable for our client and their customers.
