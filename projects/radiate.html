Radiate

A group of 8 female college students from Women of Rewriting the Code launched a passion project aimed to help promote minority owned small businesses. I designed our website, Radiate, for consumers to explore minority owned small businesses across the country by emphasizing the stories behind businesses and how we can support them.

ROLE:  Product Designer
TEAM:   Dena Sabha
Duation:   July 2020 - August 2020 (2 months)
METHODS:   User Research, Web Design, Prototyping, Animation
TOOLS:   Figma, Adobe Creative Suite

BACKGROUND

The pandemic has been culturally devastating for communities. Hundreds of thousands of small businesses closed forever and to say the pandemic has left a crushing impact on small business minority owners is an understatement. We wanted to create a platform that allows people to search for and explore small businesses who are owned specifically by women, LGBTQIA+, people of color, or people with disabilities. Behind these businesses are real people with stories to tell, and currently there are no platforms that connect customers and businesses beyond the surface level.

By May, one hundred thousand small businesses permanently closed due to the pandemic.

CHALLENGE

Currently websites like Yelp, provide an abundance of information on stores based on reviews and ratings. These social networking sites drive consumers to explore based on reviews and ratings which do not emphasize business stories.

OUTCOME

A website that offers customers the chance to explore and support minority owned small businesses by exploring a consolidated list of small businesses and learning the stories behind each busines.

OUR SOLUTION

Encouraging users to explore small businesses

Radiate's landing screen consists of three hero images to evoke emotions and demonstrate value propositions. The affordances and animations encourage users to keep scrolling to learn more about stories behind small businesses and how they can support them.

Engage users to read stories and learn ways they can support

Delightful interactions on each page to engage the user in the story behind each small business. Convey detailed information about each business and encourage the user to learn how they can support each small businesses.

Spotlight small businesses

Small businesses are grouped by categories and encourage exploration of the small businesses through a carousel. A different small business is spotlighted each week for users to have multiple points of interactions with stories.

USER RESEARCH

Through short interviews, we uncovered that people are eager to support minority owned small businesses but difficulties stemmed from not being able to find and learn about the small businesses. We surveyed over 60 people to uncover more painpoints and opportunities for our platform to address them. Social media was a very popular platform where people learned about struggling small businesses and ways they could support them. However, determining directions, hours, and other small businesses near-by would be a diligent process. There are many resources online that most people used such as Yelp, Google, etc. but more often than not, small businesses are overshadowed by large corporations.

PAINPOINTS

  • Small businesses are overshadowed by large corporations
  • No consolidated list of minority owned small businesses
  • No knowledge on story and mission statement behind a business
  • Small businesses are not advertised heavily
  • Little to no information on how to support
  • Tend to turn to convenient options like Yelp

STYLE GUIDE

The goal of this platform is to create a spotlight on small businesses by coming together as a community. With our mission in mind, I designed a logo to reflect the power of everyone from different backgrounds coming together as a community.

We wanted to create a warm and confident design and choose to use a light blue background paired with a navy blue foreground and muted red.​We choose to use different shade so blue to emphasize the sites's reliability and supportive nature. The red was chosen to reflect encouragement and passion.

LO-FIDELITY

We began our ideation by conducting a Figma brainstorming session with our engineers and each person designed a lo-fidelity mockup of what their vision for the website.

MID-FIDELITY

The design team explored various color schemes and developed mid-fidelity mockups from our early designs. We presented these to users for usability testing and to better understand their needs.

FINAL DESIGN

I designed high-fidelity prototypes based on the needs and painpoints discovered during usability testing sessions. I developed animations and interaction points to bring joy to the user experience.

REFLECTION

Moving forward, I would like to conduct more usability tests on this product and interview more small business owners and develop the designs further to address user and small business needs. Creating Radiate was a rewarding experience and I really enjoyed collaborating with students with diverse backgrounds to develop a concept we are all passionate about.
